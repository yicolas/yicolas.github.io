SEDIMENT PELLET PRESS

Overview

Many attempts have been made across multiple groups to develop a portable and reliable in the field pellet press for protrable XRF and other gepchemical analyses. From carjacks to hydraulic presses most choices are cumbersome and time consuming. Here we introduce a cheap, reproducable, lightweight and durable pellet press for use by hand or caulk gun.
This model was made specifically for pXRF measurements of fresh clays and fine sands, and will extrude and drain water as the pellets are pressed and work best after drying.

Usage

Sediments to be used in the press should first be homogenized to a consistent grain size using a mortar and pestle to reduce matrix effects.
The sediment press is composed of 5 pieces:
1) a base cylinder with a small circular relief that connects with the sediment holding cylinder
2) the sediment holding cylinder
3) a narrow dowel that compresses the homogenized sediment in the sediment chamber,
4) a dowel topper that can be compressed either by hand or using caulk gun and 5) caulk gun if desired.
Pieces are first assembled with sediment inside and then are entered into the cock gun as such: [1 2 <(sediment inside) 3 4]
After pellets are pressed, thoroughly clean the equipment and leave the pellets out to dry out as water will increase matrix effects.

Research Applications

The sediment press is especially useful for producing consistent XRF tablets from unconsolidated sediments with minimal sample preparation. This allows rapid geochemical analysis in the field for applications such as environmental monitoring, sediment provenance studies, real-time stratigraphic logging during coring or drilling, and quick assessment of clay/sand mineralogy. The press is also adaptable for use in micro-XRF and other analytical techniques requiring compacted sample pellets, opening possibilities for high-resolution mapping and low-cost field campaigns. Pellets are sized a such for use on a XXX XRF machine.
